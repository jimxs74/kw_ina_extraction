The execution of a project involves multiple parties that are obligated by a contract. Often, problems arise during the implementation phase that are not explicitly addressed in the contract or demand comprehensive analysis to resolve. These issues are formally communicated and documented throughout the duration of the project, typically in written correspondence. The most prevalent form of formal written communication is a letter, which generally raises an issue or provides a response to a problem. Projects can involve a considerable amount of correspondence, potentially reaching thousands of letters. Extracting keywords from these letters can provide a comprehensive understanding of the project's issue.

In this study, an experiment on keyword extraction from a collection of letters will be conducted. If we can accurately extract keywords from the communication history of project stakeholders, we will have a better understanding of how the project has progressed, which will help determine the next steps in project execution. This will greatly assist the project management team in prioritizing problem-solving and creating an execution plan for the project

The corpus used is in Bahasa Indonesia. Therefore, several techniques, such as stop word removal, POS tagging, and Word2Vec modeling, were adjusted to suit Bahasa Indonesia. The stop word base used in this study was obtained from previous studies conducted, the stopword list are Tala and Sastrawi
